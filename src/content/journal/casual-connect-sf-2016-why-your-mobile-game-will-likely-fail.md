[Scott Foe](http://scottfoe.blogspot.com/) recently asked me to speak at Casual Connect 2016. Given that I've had some time off, I figured it'd be a great opportunity to do a bit of navel gazing and perhaps impart some wisdom based on my most recent work experiences. After some thought, I asked [Ethan](https://gamingunicorn.com/) to join me as I value his insights and figured it would make for a fun project for us. What I didn't realize is that the talk was only 25 minutes, which led to some serious editing and a rather brisk flow. However, it all came together pretty well and I'm definitely proud of the result.

You can check out the talk below. It's a fairly high-level overview with a pretty clickbait-y title. We made the decision to keep it that way as we didn't know who the audience might be (devs, publishing folks, ad networks, etc).

In the future, I think it might be fun to create a format based solely on the quiz format that was featured on one of our slides. Something for next year, maybe...

Casual Connect is definitely pretty business-focussed. Attendance at the talks seemed on the sparse side as people opted to, uh, connect instead. Still, there was a decent turnout for our talk and I got to see some very good speakers in a more intimate setting. Two of my favorites that I saw:


 	- Neil Young on ["Everything You Know is Wrong"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W-EDemOWRZ8)  - great talk on why he thinks VR is not ready for prime time and why people are underestimating the mobile market.

 	- Robin Hunicke on ["Creatively Casual Games"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FXb6owZnqcU) - really refreshing approach from the mind that brought you _Journey_.



Oh, and I also spoke on a panel that was hosted by the inimitable [Dean Takahashi](https://twitter.com/deantak). You can [check out the transcript of the session here](http://venturebeat.com/2016/07/24/why-its-hard-to-go-from-building-games-on-console-to-mobile/view-all/) and picture is above..
